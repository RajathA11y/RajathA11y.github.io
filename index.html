A11y pitstop: introduction

Hello Guest and welcome to Accessibility pitstop. You may wonder there are a myriad resources out there and this supply here is a meare infinitesimal cluster.So, what makes this special? Or what is in it for you? I am trying to build a repository, an accessibility handbook if you may call it so.

How did this thought inspire? While trying to test among the various combinations of assistive technologies, browsers, OS platforms, devices and lot more at times we aren’t sure of how a particular A.T., would behave with an element in a combination. Thus I decided to create a resource which can be accessed across platforms just to serve as a confirmation.

What does it contain? Contains a brief intro about different screen readers, examples of various HTML elements, wcag guidelines, and many more…

Ps: this site is still a work in progress and by no meanse a complete material
